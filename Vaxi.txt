What is COVID-19?
COVID-19 is a respiratory disease caused by SARS-CoV-2.
How does COVID-19 spread?
The virus spreads mainly from person to person through respiratory droplets.
What are the symptoms of COVID-19?
Possible symptoms include: Fever, cough, shortness of breath, and fatigue.
How to prevent COVID-19?
Be vaccinated, wear a mask, and stay away from people infected with COVID-19.
What is a vaccine?
A preparation that stimulates the body's immune response against diseases.
How do COVID-19 vaccines work?
They train the immune system by acting like the virus.
Is vaccination mandatory?
No, but it is highly encouraged by the government.
Are the vaccines safe?
Yes. They were evaluated in clinical trials and met FDA standards.
Are there risks when given the vaccine?
Yes but it is extremely rare. The benefits outweigh the risks.
What are the vaccines available?
Pfizer, Moderna, Sinovac, and AstraZeneca are the common ones.
What are the side effects after vaccination?
Common ones are pain at the injection site, fever, fatigue, and headache.
